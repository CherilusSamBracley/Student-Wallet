Student Loan Forgiveness Requirements You Might Not Know About

Find out if you qualify for federal student loan debt relief

  • Student loans are a type of loan that is meant to aid students in college/university by financially assisting them with tuition, books, room and board.
  • The types of student loans include direct subsidized loans, direct unsubsidized loans, direct PLUS loans, and direct consolidation loans.
  • For direct subsidized loans, the interest is paid for by the government during the remainder that the student is in school.


  • For direct unsubsidized loans, you are responsible for paying the interest during the entire period
  • Direct Plus loans are made by the Department of Education to students and parents. The borrower is fully responsible for paying the interest.
  • Consolidation loans are a combination of one or more loans into a single loan. Its main purpose is to lower your monthly payment.
  • Through the Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF) program, borrowers who work for non-profit organizations, the armed forces, or local, state, or federal governments may be eligible to have all of their student loans forgiven.
  • Most borrowers who apply can expect relief within six weeks.
  • If a student has received a Pell Grant and meets all other requirements, they can receive up to $20,000.
  • If a student is not a Pell Grant recipient, they may receive up to $10,000.
